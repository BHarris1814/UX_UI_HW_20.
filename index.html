    Department Of Education Website Redesign Case Study

    The Discovery

    THE PROBLEM: The Department of Education current website, prevents the diverse users of educators, parents and students from easily navigating, searching for and accessing important information. The complicated layout, sections and overwhelming amount of links sends the user on a confusing journey to find the information.  In addition, the color and text failed the accessibility test limiting usability for individuals with disabilities. 

    The data collected from a random sample list: only 49% of the users were successful navigating the task on the current website.

    THE SOLUTION: Re-design the current U.S. Department of Education website by conducting user testing on interface functionality, heuristic color accessibility testing, style design and simplified the categories for more user friendly interface.

    Define and Ideate

    After running the Current Department Of Education color palette, only one color passed the accessibility test. Therefore, we conducted and tested other colors and implemented a new color palette and Typography.  

    The data collected from our user testing of the orignial Department of Education website, lead us to card sort creating a sitemap and new navigation. Allowing an easier and more comprehensivable navigation of content.

    Prototype and Testing

    The redesign of the Department of Education increased the user ability functions for task 1 and 2 from the initial Department of Education website by 47%. Overall the user functionality from the redesign of the site rose by 38% from the initial Department of Education website.
